DTC/Checkout Page

Convert viewers into customers with optimized pricing and package options

1 What is a DTC/Checkout Page?

The Direct-to-Checkout (DTC) page is where customers land after clicking the buy button on your VSL. This page displays all your package options, pricing, and the order form in one seamless experience.

Unlike traditional e-commerce where customers browse multiple products, the DTC page focuses solely on converting the customer on YOUR product with strategic pricing psychology and package positioning.

Why DTC Works: By presenting all options upfront with clear value propositions, you remove decision friction and guide customers toward your most profitable package (typically the "Best Value" 3-6 bottle option).

5 Required Elements Checklist

  • 3 Package Options: Basic, Best Value (highlighted), Premium
  • Clear Pricing: Show per-bottle price and total savings
  • Product Images: High-quality images for each package option
  • Highlight Best Value: Visual emphasis on recommended package
  • Savings Badges: Show dollar amount saved on bulk packages
  • Package Benefits: List what's included in each option
  • Simple Order Form: Minimal fields, clear labels
  • Trust Badges: Security, guarantee, shipping, reviews
  • Money-Back Guarantee: Prominently displayed with terms
  • Secure Payment Icons: SSL badge, accepted payment methods
  • Clear CTA Button: "Complete Order" or similar action-oriented text

6 Package Positioning Strategy

Why the Middle Option Wins

The "Best Value" package (middle option) should account for 50-60% of sales. Here's how to position it:

Package Positioning Strategy Expected Sales %
Basic (1-2 bottles) Entry point, no emphasis, standard pricing 15-20%
Best Value (3-6 bottles) Highlighted, labeled "BEST VALUE", bonus items included, moderate discount 50-60%
Premium (6+ bottles) Maximum savings, bulk positioning, highest discount per unit 25-30%
The Decoy Effect: The Basic package serves as a "decoy" to make the Best Value package look like a better deal. The Premium package makes the Best Value seem reasonable, not excessive.

7 Best Practices

  • Mobile-First Design: 60%+ of orders come from mobile - test thoroughly
  • One-Click Package Selection: Don't make customers navigate to new pages
  • Show Savings Clearly: "$120 savings" is more compelling than "42% off"
  • Add Bonuses to Middle Tier: Free guides, extra bottles, etc. increase perceived value
  • Minimize Form Fields: Only ask for essential information
  • Use Progress Indicators: Show customers where they are in the checkout process
  • Display Multiple Payment Options: Credit card, PayPal, etc.
  • A/B Test Everything: Headlines, prices, package names, button colors

8 Common Mistakes to Avoid

Warning: These mistakes can cut your conversion rate in half.
  • Too Many Options: More than 3 packages creates decision paralysis
  • No Clear Recommendation: Customers need guidance - highlight your best value
  • Hidden Costs: Surprise shipping fees at checkout kill conversions
  • Complex Forms: Every additional field reduces completion by 5-10%
  • Weak Guarantee: "30-day money back" is less effective than "60-day"
  • No Trust Signals: Missing security badges increase abandonment
  • Unclear Pricing: Customers should instantly understand cost per bottle
  • Poor Mobile Experience: Broken mobile checkout = 60% revenue loss

Performance Metrics & Benchmarks

Checkout Conversion Rate: 30-50% of people who land on this page should complete purchase. Below 25% indicates problems.
Best Value Package Selection: Should account for 50-60% of all orders. If under 40%, your positioning needs work.
Cart Abandonment Rate: Expect 30-40% abandonment. Higher rates indicate friction in your checkout process.
Average Order Value (AOV): With proper package positioning, AOV should be $150-250 for supplement offers.
Mobile vs Desktop Conversion: Mobile should convert at 70-80% the rate of desktop. Lower indicates mobile UX issues.
Form Completion Time: Should take 2-3 minutes average. Longer times suggest form complexity issues.
