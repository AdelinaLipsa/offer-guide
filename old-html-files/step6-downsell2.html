Downsell 2: Smaller Complement

Final recovery offer with trial or starter size of complementary product

1 What is Downsell 2?

This is your FINAL attempt to add value to the order. When customers decline Upsell 2 (the full complementary product), you present this last offer: a smaller, trial-sized version of that complementary product at an incredibly low price.

The strategy here is simple: make the offer so small and inexpensive that it's almost silly to say no. Position it as "just try it" rather than a full commitment.

Final Recovery: 10-20% of customers who declined Upsell 2 will accept this downsell. While each sale is smaller ($19-$27), it's pure additional revenue and introduces customers to the complementary product line.

3 Required Elements Checklist

  • "Final Offer" Headline: Make it crystal clear this is the last chance
  • Trial/Starter Positioning: Position as "just try it" rather than full commitment
  • Extremely Low Price: $19-27 range - almost impulse purchase level
  • Small Quantity: Single bottle or trial size
  • Comparison to Declined Offer: Show how much smaller/cheaper this is
  • Heavy Discount: Show 50-70% savings from regular price
  • Remove All Risk: Emphasize guarantee, free shipping, one-click
  • Absolute Finality: "Never offered again" messaging
  • Social Proof: Quick testimonial about trying it
  • Strong Yes Button: "Just let me try it for $19"
  • Neutral No Button: Simple "Complete my order" (not negative)

4 Pricing Strategy

The $19 Sweet Spot: $19 is psychologically significant. It's under $20 which feels like "almost nothing." It's less than a meal, less than a movie ticket. This price point maximizes impulse acceptance.

5 Key Messaging Strategy

Focus on "Try" Not "Buy"

Every element should reduce commitment anxiety:

  • "Just try it and see for yourself"
  • "Risk-free trial size"
  • "Perfect way to test it out"
  • "See if it works for you"
  • "If you love it, order more later"
  • "No commitment beyond this one bottle"

Emphasize the Absurdly Low Price

  • "Less than $1 per day"
  • "Less than a coffee"
  • "Cheaper than lunch"
  • "Only $19 to find out if it works"
  • "Almost silly to say no at this price"

6 Best Practices

  • Keep Price Under $30: Above $30, it stops feeling like an impulse buy
  • Single Bottle Only: Don't offer multiple quantities - keep it simple
  • Emphasize "Final": Make it clear the funnel ends after this
  • Use Trial Language: "Trial," "starter," "sample" reduce commitment fear
  • Show Massive Discount: 50-70% off retail makes it irresistible
  • No Pressure Decline: At this point, let them go gracefully
  • One Last Testimonial: Someone who "almost skipped it" and loved it
  • Keep It Short: Don't over-explain - simple and quick decision

7 Common Mistakes to Avoid

Warning: This is your last chance - don't blow it with these mistakes.
  • Still Too Expensive: Above $30 kills the impulse buy psychology
  • Multiple Options: Keep it to ONE simple trial offer
  • Not Emphasizing "Final": Customers need to know this is the last offer
  • Overselling: Keep copy short - don't make them overthink
  • Aggressive Decline Language: At this point, respect their decision
  • No Social Proof: Need that "I almost skipped this" testimonial
  • Complex Positioning: Should be dead simple: "Try 1 bottle for $19"
  • Missing Finality Cues: Make it clear they're heading to thank you page next

8 Strategic Purpose

Beyond Immediate Revenue

This downsell serves multiple purposes:

  • Revenue Recovery: Capture 10-20% who would otherwise decline everything
  • Product Introduction: Get complementary product into their hands
  • Future Sales: If they like it, they'll reorder at full price
  • Email Segmentation: Now you know they're interested in both product lines
  • Reduced Acquisition Cost: Every $19 helps offset ad spend
Long-term Value: 30-40% of customers who accept this trial offer become repeat buyers of the complementary product line. You're not just making $19 - you're potentially creating a multi-product customer.

Performance Metrics & Benchmarks

Acceptance Rate: 10-20% of those who declined Upsell 2. Top performers hit 25%.
