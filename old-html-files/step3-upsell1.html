Upsell 1: More Quantity

Increase order value by offering additional bottles at a special discount

1 What is Upsell 1?

Immediately after a customer completes their checkout, they see this "interruption" page offering them additional bottles of the SAME product at a discounted rate - but only if they add it to their order RIGHT NOW.

This is your first opportunity to increase the average order value (AOV). Since the customer just committed to buying your product, they're in a "buying mindset" and are more likely to accept this upgrade.

Why This Works: Customers have already overcome their purchase objections and entered payment info. Adding to their order with one click is psychologically easier than making a new purchase later. Acceptance rates of 20-35% are common.

3 Required Elements Checklist

  • Interruption Headline: "WAIT!" or similar to grab attention
  • VIP/Exclusive Positioning: Make it feel special and time-limited
  • Clear Value Proposition: Explain what they're getting
  • Product Image: Same product they just bought
  • Price Comparison: Show regular price vs. discounted price
  • Savings Calculator: Show exact dollar amount saved
  • Benefit List: Why they should accept this offer
  • Urgency Element: Timer or "one-time only" messaging
  • Strong Yes Button: Action-oriented with price included
  • Weak No Button: De-emphasized decline option
  • One-Click Acceptance: No need to re-enter payment info

4 Pricing Strategy

Original Package Upsell Offer Discount Level
1 Bottle ($69) Add 2 more bottles for $98 (total: 3 bottles) 30% off per bottle
3 Bottles ($147) Add 3 more bottles for $117 (total: 6 bottles) 40% off per bottle
6 Bottles ($234) Add 6 more bottles for $180 (total: 12 bottles) 50% off per bottle
Discount Sweet Spot: Offer 30-50% off the regular per-bottle price. Less than 30% doesn't feel special enough. More than 50% can raise suspicion about your regular pricing.

5 Psychology & Positioning

Why Customers Accept Upsells

  • Already in Buying Mode: They just made a purchase decision
  • Sunk Cost Fallacy: They've invested time and already entered payment info
  • Fear of Missing Out (FOMO): "One-time only" creates urgency
  • Desire to Maximize Results: More product = better/faster results
  • Perceived Value: Significant discount makes it feel like a smart decision

Key Messaging Points

Your copy should emphasize:

  • This discount is ONLY available right now on this page
  • Customers who buy more see better/faster results
  • Free/combined shipping (one delivery)
  • One-click convenience (no re-entering information)
  • Same guarantee applies to the upsell
  • The regular price shows this is a special deal

6 Best Practices

  • Keep It Simple: One offer only - don't confuse with multiple options
  • Make "Yes" Prominent: Large, colorful button with clear benefit
  • De-emphasize "No": Smaller, less prominent decline option
  • Use Negative Decline Copy: "No thanks, I don't want to save..." reminds them of loss
  • Add Social Proof: "87% of customers add this to their order"
  • Show Timer: 5-10 minute countdown creates urgency
  • Emphasize Convenience: "One-click" and "no re-entering info"
  • Test Different Quantities: Try offering 2, 3, or 4 additional bottles

7 Common Mistakes to Avoid

Warning: These mistakes dramatically reduce acceptance rates.
  • Weak Discount: Less than 25% off feels insignificant
  • Too Expensive: Upsell shouldn't exceed original order value by 2x
  • Multiple Options: Keep it to ONE offer - choices reduce conversions
  • No Urgency: Without time pressure, customers defer decisions
  • Requires Re-Entry: Making customers enter payment again kills conversions
  • Different Product: Save cross-sells for Upsell 2 - keep this same product
  • Weak Headlines: Generic copy doesn't grab attention
  • No Price Comparison: Show the savings clearly with before/after prices

Performance Metrics & Benchmarks

Acceptance Rate: 20-35% is the industry standard. Top performers achieve 40-50%.
AOV Increase: Successful Upsell 1 should increase your average order value by 25-40%.
Revenue Impact: If 30% accept and upsell is $120, you add $36 per customer on average.
Decline Rate Impact: Don't worry about declines - they still complete their original order. Declines have minimal effect on refunds.
Mobile vs Desktop: Mobile acceptance rates run 10-15% lower - optimize mobile experience.
Page Load Time: Every 1 second delay costs 5-10% in acceptance - keep it fast!
