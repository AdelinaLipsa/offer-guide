Downsell 1: Smaller Quantity

Recover customers who declined your first upsell with a more attractive offer

1 What is Downsell 1?

When a customer clicks "No Thanks" on Upsell 1, they immediately see this downsell page. Instead of letting them go, you present a SMALLER, MORE ATTRACTIVE offer that's harder to refuse.

The key difference: This offer is for fewer bottles but at an even BETTER per-bottle discount than the upsell. It removes the objection of "too expensive" while still increasing your average order value.

Recovery Strategy: 15-25% of customers who declined Upsell 1 will accept Downsell 1. This is pure profit recovery that would otherwise be lost. Even a small acceptance adds significant revenue over time.

3 Required Elements Checklist

  • "Last Chance" Headline: Create urgency without pressure
  • Empathy Message: Acknowledge their decline without making them feel bad
  • Smaller Quantity: Typically 1-2 bottles vs. 3+ in the upsell
  • Better Per-Unit Price: Lower price per bottle than the upsell
  • Comparison Visual: Show declined offer vs. current offer
  • Clear Savings: Emphasize the discount percentage (40-60%)
  • Low Commitment Angle: "Just 1 bottle" feels easy to say yes to
  • Finality Messaging: "Never offered again" creates FOMO
  • Strong Yes Button: Include price in the button text
  • Weak No Button: Frame decline as a loss

5 Why Downsells Work

Objection Removal

The downsell addresses why customers declined the upsell:

  • Price Objection: $29 feels insignificant compared to $117
  • Quantity Objection: 1 bottle is a tiny commitment
  • Decision Fatigue: Simpler choice - just one option
  • Regret Aversion: "What if I miss out?" - final offer creates FOMO

Key Messaging Points

  • We heard you - this is a better fit
  • Absolute lowest price ever offered
  • Just 1 bottle - minimal commitment
  • This is your FINAL chance at this price
  • Ships free with current order
  • No-brainer addition to extend supply

6 Best Practices

  • Keep It Simple: Single offer only - don't add complexity
  • Emphasize Better Value: Lower per-bottle price than upsell
  • Show Respect: Don't make them feel bad for declining
  • Create Urgency: "Final offer" and "never again" messaging
  • Minimize Risk: Emphasize guarantee and free shipping
  • Use Smaller Numbers: "$29" feels insignificant vs. "$117"
  • Frame the Decline Negatively: "No thanks, I'll pay full price later"
  • Test Different Quantities: Try 1 bottle vs. 2 bottles

7 Common Mistakes to Avoid

Warning: These mistakes will kill your recovery rate.
  • Same or Worse Deal: Downsell must be MORE attractive per unit
  • Still Too Expensive: Keep total under $50 for best results
  • Shaming the Customer: Never make them feel bad for declining
  • Multiple Options: Keep it to ONE simple offer
  • No Urgency: Without finality, they'll think they can get it later
  • Complicated Messaging: Keep copy simple and benefit-focused
  • Different Product: Keep it the same product as original order
  • Weak Positioning: Must feel like an exclusive "final chance" deal

Performance Metrics & Benchmarks

Acceptance Rate: 15-25% of those who declined Upsell 1 should accept Downsell 1.
Revenue Recovery: If 300 people decline Upsell 1, and 60 accept Downsell 1 at $29, you recover $1,740.
Combined Impact: Upsell 1 (30% × $117) + Downsell 1 (17.5% × $29) = ~$40 additional per customer.
Optimal Price Point: $29-39 for single bottle downsells performs best. Higher reduces acceptance.
Page Load Speed: Keep under 2 seconds - delays kill urgency and acceptance rates.
A/B Testing Impact: Testing price points ($29 vs $35 vs $39) can change acceptance by 20-30%.
